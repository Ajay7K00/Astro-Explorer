SPACE

Space-weather, Plasma & Aeronomy Center for Exploration

Welcome!

The SPACE team is a dynamic group of physicists, PhD scholars, and research students dedicated to advancing space weather forecasting. Our mission is to enhance the protection of satellites, communication and navigation systems, power grids, and high-altitude travelers by improving early warning systems. We focus on studying solar transients near Earth, including Interplanetary Coronal Mass Ejections (ICMEs), Corotating Interaction Regions (CIRs), and Solar Energetic Particles (SEPs), to better understand their impact on Earth's magnetosphere and radiation belts. Additionally, our research explores plasma waves, instabilities, solar wind heating-cooling mechanisms using a thermodynamic approach, and particle energization processes. Join us as we explore the solar wind and its far-reaching influence on Geospace.
